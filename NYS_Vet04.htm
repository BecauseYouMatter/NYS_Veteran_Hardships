NYS Veteran Hardships by Zip Code

Overall Disability Increased, Veteran Disability Improved, and the Remaining Need Is Still Substantial

New York State’s disability count increased overall, but the ACS 5-year estimates suggest veterans were the one group that showed improvement. From 2019 to 2024, the total number of people with a disability increased by 10.55% (+216,595), and most of the growth was among nonveterans, who increased by 13.22% (+245,352). In contrast, the number of veterans with a disability decreased by 14.63% (−28,757). This is encouraging news—however, 167,819 veterans living with a disability is still a substantial number and highlights the continued need for targeted support.

Data: U.S. Census Bureau ACS 5-year estimates (2019 & 2024). Cleaned and summarized in RStudio.  |  Download: PDF

This ZIP code disability pattern looks less like a community-wide tide and more like a veteran-specific shift. When ZIP codes are grouped by whether total disability (veteran + nonveteran) increased or decreased from 2019 to 2024, the overall community trend and the veteran trend do not move together. In the 971 ZIP codes where total disability increased, nonveteran disability increased while veteran disability still declined; and in the 716 ZIP codes where total disability decreased, veteran disability also declined. Taken together, veteran disability decreased in both “improving” and “worsening” community groups, suggesting that veteran disability change is not directly explained by broader ZIP level disability movement and may reflect veteran specific factors that warrant deeper analysis.
