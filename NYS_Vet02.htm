NYS Veteran Hardships: A Zip Code Lens

Poverty and Disability: The Structural Barriers to Employment

Labor force participation is not just an “economic” measure—it is shaped by systemic barriers that can prevent veterans from seeing themselves as employable or staying connected to the workforce. Two of the most critical barriers are poverty and disability, both of which severely limit access to transportation, stable housing, healthcare, and consistent work opportunities.

The four interactive maps below provide a high-resolution, statewide view of these barriers at the ZIP-code level. By comparing the veteran community side-by-side with the community as a whole, we can identify where these hardships are disproportionately concentrated.

  • Analyze the Contrast: Use the maps to see if veteran hardship mirrors the local area or represents a unique challenge in that ZIP code.

  • Track the Shift: Use the slider to compare 2019 and 2024 and observe how these barriers have evolved over the post-COVID landscape.

Each map opens on the full state of New York, but you can pan/zoom into specific regions. Use the filter control to switch the ACS 5-year between 2019 and 2024 to see how patterns shift over time.
