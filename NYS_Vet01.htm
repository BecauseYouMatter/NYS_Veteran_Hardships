NYS Veteran Hardships: A Zip Code Lens

The State Perspective: Veterans Are Hitting the "Workforce Wall" Hardest

New York State's labor force is shrinking overall, but a closer look at the data reveals an alarming disparity. The decline is falling disproportionately on the veteran community, signaling a shift that regional averages fail to capture.

From 2019 to 2024, the Total Labor Force decreased by 0.45%. However, when we segment the data:

  • Non-Veterans: Decreased by only 0.14% (9,133,503 to 9,120,978).
  • Veterans: Decreased by a staggering 13.0% (225,290 to 196,011).

This 13% decline—nearly 100 times the rate of non-veterans—highlights a much sharper contraction for veterans than for the state overall. This data sets the stage for our geographic analysis: if the workforce is shrinking this rapidly, where are these veterans going, and are they falling into hardship?

A Widespread Decline: Veterans Left Behind in Growing Communities

The reduction in the veteran labor force is strikingly widespread and disconnected from local economic growth. By grouping New York's ZIP codes into those that grew, declined, or remained stable, we can see a troubling constant: Veterans lost ground in every single category.

As shown in the analysis below, even in the 769 ZIP codes where the total labor force increased by over 350,000 people, the veteran population within those same areas still saw a net loss.

  • In Declining ZIPs: Total labor force dropped by ~387,000; Veterans accounted for over 23,000 of that loss.

  • In Growing ZIPs: Total labor force increased by ~359,000; yet Veterans still saw a net decrease of over 5,000.

The Visual Evidence: In the dot plots below, notice that the "trail lines" are significantly longer in the horizontal direction (Veteran change) than the vertical (Non-Veteran change). This visually confirms that the veteran decline is not simply a reflection of local economic conditions, but a broader, systemic pattern affecting veterans across the entire state, regardless of how well their local economy is performing.

The table below provides a high-resolution look at the poverty counts for every ZIP code in New York State. By moving away from broad regional summaries, this index allows you to see the exact shifts occurring in individual neighborhoods between 2019 and 2024.

Each entry includes micro-visualizations (to-scale horizontal bars) that represent the population of veterans and non-veterans. This layout is designed for rapid skimming to identify:
