NYS Veteran Hardships: A Zip Code Lens

Moving Beyond Regional Averages to Identify Real Need

While regional reports often group diverse communities into broad "buckets," those averages can hide the true struggles of our veterans. This interactive study applies a microscope to New York State, disaggregating Labor Force Participation, Poverty, and Disability down to the ZIP code level.

Why this matters now:
Using the latest ACS 5-year data, this project provides a comprehensive look at how veteran demographics shifted between 2019 (Pre Pandemic) and 2024 (Post Pandemic). With the next full Census results not due until 2031, this is our most accurate tool for understanding the post-COVID landscape.

1. Where are Veterans in the Labor Force?

As New York's workforce evolves, veteran participation varies drastically by neighborhood. A "single-digit" regional average can often mask specific communities where the labor force is actually in crisis.

  • Explore the Data: Use the map below to zoom into your specific region.

  • Compare the Shift: Use the filter panel to toggle between 2019 and 2024 to see how your community has changed over the last five years.

2. Contextualizing the Workforce

To understand if a veteran’s struggle is unique or a reflection of their local economy, we must look at the broader picture. This map shows the Total Labor Force Participation by ZIP code.

By comparing the Veteran map to the General Population map, we can identify "Hardship Gaps"—areas where veterans are struggling even when the local economy is active, or rural pockets where the entire community is being left behind by regional hubs.

  • Identify Discrepancies: Does the veteran participation match the local trend, or is there a specific barrier for the veteran community?

  • Pinpoint Isolation: See where rural ZIP codes are "hidden" by the economic success of nearby hubs like Fort Drum or Plattsburgh.
